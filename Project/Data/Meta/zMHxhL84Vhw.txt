Arkarna, you say? Who?? Well, they're the biggest dance-guitar band you might have never heard of. The band first emerged in 1997 with the critically-acclaimed, platinum selling debut album Fresh Meat, which spawned 4 singles and chart success not just in the UK but also internationally.

Warp forward and the original band line up has changed and streamlined and now consists of Ollie J, founding member, singer and precocious engineering prodigy who cut his teeth working with the likes with Leftfield (mixing their seminal album Leftism), The Prodigy and Madonna, who is joined by classically trained Goldsmith Uni Grad, guitarist and songwriter Matt Hart. Another change is that the Warners situation is no longer and the band are flexing their independent arm and will be releasing material under their own steam.

For the past three years, the pair have hunkered down in their north west London studio testing out new material and now, they've fine tuned a bag of storming compositions which will be sewn together to make up the brand new album due to be unleashed to the unsuspecting public in 2012.

The first taster from the album is the synth driven pulsing monster that is 'Left Is Best'. The track encapsulates the new Arkarna energy and sets out their stall with full effect. The offering comes with a package of remixes to please and delight - with credits going to the trendsetter DJ favourites So Called Scumbags, dubstep man-of-the-moment Rednek as well as a beating up from Arkarna themselves. Yes, Arkarna are back to claim their space on the dancefloor, moshpit and in discerning music lovers' record collections the planet over. No-one else sounds quite like Arkarna.

'Left Is Best' will be release in January - setting the scene very nicely for an interesting 2012 for Arkarna.
